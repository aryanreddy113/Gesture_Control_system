Gesture Control Virtual System

Transforming Human Gestures into Digital Commands

Problem Statement

Traditional input devices like mice and keyboards, while effective, present limitations in specific modern scenarios:

Hygiene & Health: In public kiosks or medical environments, physical contact with surfaces can spread pathogens.

Workflow Efficiency: In creative or presentation-heavy roles, switching between physical tools and screen interaction can break "flow."

Hardware Dependency: Physical peripherals are prone to mechanical wear and require specific surfaces to operate effectively.

Proposed Solution

Our project introduces a Vision-Based Gesture Control System that replaces the physical mouse with intuitive hand movements. By leveraging Computer Vision and MediaPipe's hand-tracking capabilities, the system maps real-time coordinates to system-level mouse events.

Core Functionalities:

Cursor Movement: Smooth tracking of the index finger (or hand centroid) to control the XY coordinates of the pointer.

Clicking Mechanism: Detection of a specific gesture like hand pinching is included for selection.

Vertical Scrolling: Using specific hand gestures to simulate the scroll wheel for navigating long documents or webpages.

Gestures Used

☝️ Cursor Movement: Index Finger

⬆️ Scroll Up: Thumb + Little Finger

⬇️ Scroll Down: Thumb Finger

🤏 Click (Pinching): Thumb + Index Finger

🤟 Screenshot: Thumb index finger + little finger
